About me

I am a Ritt assistant professor (postdoc) at Columbia University, and also a June Huh visiting fellow at the Center for Mathematical Challenges in KIAS. My field of interest is algebraic geometry.

I often write my name as Yoonjoo Kim without a dash.

From Fall 2022 to Spring 2023, I was a postdoc at Universität Bonn in Daniel Huybrechts' group (ERC HyperK team).

Before that, I was a PhD student at Stony Brook University. My advisor was Radu Laza.

Email: yk3029 [AT] columbia [DOT] edu.

Research

I work on the following topics in algebraic geometry.
  • Abelian fibrations (degeneration of abelian varieties). These include Lagrangian fibrations, Hitchin fibrations, and abelian fibrations of Calabi-Yau varieties. Related keywords are:
    • Néron models, group scheme actions, and δ-regularity
    • Hodge structures and Ngô support theorem
    • Dual abelian fibrations and Fourier-Mukai transforms
  • Global geometry and topology of symplectic varieties. These include abelian varieties, compact hyper-Kähler manifolds, and primitive/irreducible symplectic varieties. Related keywords are:
    • Global constructions of symplectic varieties
    • Mumford-Tate and Looijenga-Lunts-Verbitsky groups
    • Chow motives
